This type of discomfort can sometimes be related to the heart not getting enough oxygen during exertion, which is commonly known as angina. The fact that it hasn’t been getting worse and doesn’t come with other symptoms like shortness of breath or dizziness is somewhat reassuring, but it’s still important to address.

What often happens in cases like this is that the blood vessels supplying the heart may be narrowed, so when you’re active, the heart needs more oxygen and can’t always get enough, leading to that sensation of tightness. 

My recommendation is that you avoid pushing yourself with strenuous activity until we can look into this further. It would be wise to arrange for some additional evaluation—often this means an EKG and possibly a stress test—to better understand what’s going on.
